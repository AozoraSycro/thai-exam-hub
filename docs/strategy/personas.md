# PERSONA PACKAGE: Thai Exam Hub
**Research Basis**: Synthesis of Thai educational landscape (TCAS), behavioral patterns of Gen Z students, and private tutoring market trends.
**Segments Identified**: 3 Personas

---

## PERSONA 1: น้องฟ้า (Nong Fah)
**Archetype**: The High-Stakes Sprinter (ม.6 Crammer)
**Quote**: "เหลือเวลาอีกแค่ 3 เดือน แต่หนูยังเก็บเนื้อหาไม่ครบเลยค่ะ เครียดมาก กลัวสอบไม่ติดคณะในฝัน"

### Who They Are
*   **Role**: Grade 12 (ม.6) student in the Science-Math or Arts-Language track.
*   **Experience**: Currently in the "final boss" stage of high school.
*   **Team size**: Studying solo but active in Twitter (X) #TCAS study communities.
*   **Tools they use daily**: Twitter (for news), Clear (app for notes), YouTube (tutorial videos), TikTok (study hacks).

### Goals
*   **Primary job goal**: Clear the TGAT/TPAT and A-Level exams with high enough scores for their target faculty.
*   **Personal ambition**: To get into a Top 3 university (Chula, Thammasat, Mahidol) and make their parents proud.
*   **Success looks like**: Seeing a "Selected" status on the TCAS dashboard.

### Frustrations
*   **Day-to-day pains**: Time is running out; there's too much to read and not enough hours.
*   **What keeps them up at night**: The fear of "หลุด" (failing to get a seat) and having to take a gap year.
*   **Previous failed solutions**: Buying thick textbooks they never finished; paying for expensive courses that are too long for their remaining time.

### Buying Behavior
*   **How they discover tools**: Viral tweets, "Studygram" influencers, or friends in the same cram school.
*   **Who influences their decisions**: Top-tier tutors and peers who have high mock scores.
*   **How they evaluate solutions**: Does it have real past papers? Are the explanations fast and easy to understand?
*   **Budget authority**: Requests money from parents (needs to justify the value/success rate).
*   **Devices used**: **Heavy Mobile/Tablet**. Uses iPhone for quick quizzes during commutes and iPad for intensive mock exam sessions.

### Jobs-to-be-Done
*   **Functional job**: Practice past exams and identify weak points instantly.
*   **Emotional job**: Reduce anxiety by seeing incremental progress in mock scores.
*   **Social job**: Be the student who "cracked the code" despite starting late.

### Voice Samples
*   "ขอพิกัดโจทย์แนวสปีดเทสต์หน่อยค่ะ พี่ๆ แนะนำเว็บไหนดี?"
*   "วิชาเคมี A-Level ปีล่าสุดยากมาก ใครมีเฉลยละเอียดบ้าง?"
*   "จะเก็บไทย-สังคมยังไงให้ทันใน 2 อาทิตย์คะ?"

## PERSONA 2: น้องพีช (Nong Peach)
**Archetype**: The Early Bird Planner (ม.4-5 Student)
**Quote**: "อยากเริ่มเก็บเนื้อหาและลองทำโจทย์ตั้งแต่เนิ่นๆ จะได้ไม่เหนื่อยตอน ม.6 ค่ะ"

### Who They Are
*   **Role**: Grade 10-11 (ม.4-5) student.
*   **Experience**: Fresh into the high school curriculum, still exploring interests.
*   **Team size**: Influenced by "Studygrams" and high-achieving school seniors.
*   **Tools they use daily**: Instagram (Studygrams), Notion (for organization), GoodNotes.

### Goals
*   **Primary job goal**: Master the foundation of core subjects (Math, English, Science) before the pressure hits.
*   **Personal ambition**: To be "chill" in ม.6 because the hard work was done early.
*   **Success looks like**: Consistently high GPAX and feeling confident when looking at TGAT questions.

### Frustrations
*   **Day-to-day pains**: School assignments take up a lot of time; hard to balance school work with TCAS prep.
*   **What keeps them up at night**: Seeing ม.6 seniors struggling and fearing they will end up the same way.
*   **Previous failed solutions**: Randomly searching for exams online but finding they are outdated or too difficult for their current level.

### Buying Behavior
*   **How they discover tools**: Searching "เตรียมตัวสอบเข้ามหาวิทยาลัย" on Google/YouTube.
*   **Who influences their decisions**: Educational YouTubers and "Pee-Rahas" (Seniors).
*   **How they evaluate solutions**: UI/UX (Must be pretty and easy to use), variety of questions, and progress tracking features.
*   **Budget authority**: Subscriptions or affordable one-time payments (sometimes uses their own pocket money).
*   **Devices used**: **Primary Tablet (iPad)**. Spends hours on the iPad for organized study, uses Smartphone for "Micro-learning" during breaks.

### Jobs-to-be-Done
*   **Functional job**: Build a habit of consistent practice without being overwhelmed.
*   **Emotional job**: Feel ahead of the curve and secure in their future.
*   **Social job**: Be seen as the "organized and prepared" student.

### Voice Samples
*   "ม.4 เริ่มเก็บเนื้อหาคณิตยังไงดีคะ?"
*   "อยากได้เว็บรวมโจทย์แยกบทค่ะ จะได้เอาไว้ทบทวนหลังเรียนจบแต่ละบทที่โรงเรียน"
*   "มี App ไหนช่วยแพลนการอ่านหนังสือบ้างไหม?"

## PERSONA 3: พี่เก่ง (P'Keng)
**Archetype**: The Tech-Savvy Mentor (Private Tutor)
**Quote**: "ผมมองหาเครื่องมือที่ช่วยให้เด็กๆ ฝึกทำโจทย์ได้ทุกที่ และผมสามารถติดตามผลได้ง่าย"

### Who They Are
*   **Role**: Part-time or Full-time Private Tutor (Freelance or Cram School owner).
*   **Experience**: 3-7 years teaching high school students.
*   **Team size**: Manages 10-50 students.
*   **Tools they use daily**: iPad/Tablet for teaching, MacBook for slide prep, Line/Messenger for student support.

### Goals
*   **Primary job goal**: Ensure their students get high scores (which builds the tutor's reputation).
*   **Personal ambition**: To scale their tutoring business and reduce time spent on manual administrative tasks (like grading).
*   **Success looks like**: 90%+ of students passing their target faculties.

### Frustrations
*   **Day-to-day pains**: Spending too much time looking for high-quality past papers; explaining the same mistakes repeatedly.
*   **What keeps them up at night**: Students not practicing enough between sessions.
*   **Previous failed solutions**: Printing paper sheets (messy and hard to track); sending PDFs in Line groups (students lose them).

### Buying Behavior
*   **How they discover tools**: Tech blogs, education seminars, or specialized tutor communities.
*   **Who influences their decisions**: Other tutors, feedback from students about what apps they actually like.
*   **How they evaluate solutions**: Quality of question bank, accuracy of keys, and "Shareability" (can I send a link to my students?).
*   **Budget authority**: Business expense (willing to pay for tools that save time or add value to their students).
*   **Devices used**: **Multi-device**. Laptop for content creation, Tablet for live teaching, Smartphone for quick communication and monitoring.

### Jobs-to-be-Done
*   **Functional job**: Provide students with a professional platform for homework and mock exams.
*   **Emotional job**: Feel like a modern, updated, and high-value educator.
*   **Social job**: Be the tutor that students recommend because "their materials/tools are the best."

### Voice Samples
*   "มีเฉลยละเอียดแบบที่เด็กอ่านแล้วเข้าใจเองได้เลยไหมครับ?"
*   "อยากได้ระบบที่แรนดอมโจทย์ให้เด็กทำเป็นชุดๆ ได้"
*   "น้องๆ ปีนี้ไม่ค่อยชอบทำโจทย์ในกระดาษแล้ว ต้องหาอะไรที่เป็น Digital ให้เขา"

---

### ICP Summary
*   **Primary ICP**: Gen Z Thai students (15-18 years old) preparing for national entrance exams.
*   **Secondary ICP**: Private tutors and small-to-medium cram schools looking for digital infrastructure.
*   **Anti-ICP**: Students looking for "leaked" exam papers or shortcuts (Thai Exam Hub is for legitimate practice).

### Messaging Implications
*   **For Nong Fah (Crammer)**: Lead with **Speed and Accuracy**. "เก็บเนื้อหาครบ จบใน 30 วัน", "เก็งแนวข้อสอบแม่นยำ".
*   **For Nong Peach (Planner)**: Lead with **Foundation and Structure**. "เตรียมตัวก่อน สบายกว่า", "คลังโจทย์แยกบท เรียนจบทำได้เลย".
*   **For P'Keng (Tutor)**: Lead with **Efficiency and Professionalism**. "ตัวช่วยติวเตอร์ยุคใหม่ ลดเวลาเตรียมสอน", "ยกระดับการสอนด้วยคลังโจทย์ดิจิทัล".
