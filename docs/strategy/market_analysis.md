# PRODUCT ANALYTICS REPORT: Thai EdTech Market Analysis (Thai Exam Hub)
**Date**: May 2024 | **Product Area**: Growth & Monetization strategy

## Executive Summary
The Thai K-12 EdTech market is highly seasonal, driven almost entirely by the **TCAS (Thai University Central Admission System)** cycle. **Thai Exam Hub** enters a competitive but fragmented space where students often juggle multiple free and paid resources. By focusing on a "Zero-Cost, Zero-Login" model, we can capture high-intent traffic during peak cramming months (October–February).

**North Star Metric**: Weekly Active Users (WAU) performing at least one full quiz.
**Success Target**: 1,500 Unique Visitors (UV)/day with an AdSense revenue goal of ฿500/week.

---

## 1. Target Audience: Who uses exam prep in Thailand?
The primary users are **Upper Secondary Students (M.4–M.6)**, with the core demographic being **M.6 students (aged 17-18)** in their final year of high school.

### User Personas
*   **The TCAS Crammer (M.6)**: Primary user. High stress, seeking quick practice, focused on TGAT/TPAT and A-Level. Uses mobile devices primarily during commute or late-night study sessions.
*   **The Early Bird (M.4-M.5)**: Secondary user. Looking for foundation building, often practicing O-NET or early subject-specific quizzes.
*   **The Supportive Parent**: High influence on paid tools, but for a free site, they act as a discovery channel (sharing links in LINE groups).
*   **The Private Tutor**: Uses free tools to assign "homework" to students. If the UX is clean, they will bookmark and repeat-use.

## 2. Seasonal Trends: The TCAS Pulse
Traffic to Thai exam sites is not linear. It follows the "TCAS Heartbeat":

| Period | Phase | Usage Level | Focus Subjects |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **May - Sep** | Semester 1 | Moderate | School midterms, foundation building. |
| **Oct - Nov** | **Pre-TGAT/TPAT** | **High** | Intensive TGAT practice (English, Critical Thinking). |
| **Dec** | **Exam Month** | **Peak** | Last-minute reviews, checking answer keys (unofficial). |
| **Jan - Feb** | **Pre-A-Level** | **Peak** | Subject-specific cramming (Math, Science, English). |
| **Mar** | **A-Level Month** | **Extreme Peak** | High-velocity practice and result prediction. |
| **Apr** | Post-Exam | Low | Students shifting to university rankings/portfolios. |

**Strategy**: Marketing efforts should quadruple in September to capture the "Panic-Study" wave starting in October.

---

## 3. Subject Demand Hierarchy
Based on university admission requirements and difficulty perception:

1.  **English (TGAT1 / A-Level English)**: Highest demand. Mandatory for almost all faculties.
2.  **Mathematics (Applied Math 1 & 2)**: Highest "pain point" subject. Students seek the most explanations here.
3.  **TGAT (General Aptitude)**: High demand because it is a "new" format and every student must take it.
4.  **A-Level Sciences (Physics, Chem, Bio)**: High demand from "สายวิทย์" (Science-stream) students targeting Medicine/Engineering.
5.  **Thai & Social Studies**: High volume but lower "intensity" as students often study these last.

## 5. Key Insights & Recommendations

1.  **Mobile-First is Mandatory**: >85% of Thai students will access this via mobile Chrome or Safari (via LINE/Facebook in-app browsers). The UI must be lightweight.
2.  **The "Answer Key" Hook**: High-intent search traffic comes from students looking for "เฉลย [Exam Year]" (Answer keys). Content strategy should prioritize recent past papers (2566, 2567).
3.  **The LINE Viral Loop**: Thai students live in LINE groups. Adding a "Share score to LINE" button is the #1 growth lever.
4.  **Dark Mode**: Many students study late at night (22:00 - 02:00). A dark mode toggle will improve D7 retention.

## Recommended Experiments
*   **Experiment 1**: Add a "Streak" counter. *Hypothesis*: Gamification increases D7 retention by 15% in the Thai market.
*   **Experiment 2**: PDF Download vs. Online Quiz. *Hypothesis*: Offering a free PDF version in exchange for a site visit increases SEO ranking for "ข้อสอบ PDF".
