## COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE REPORT
**Competitor**: Dek-D | **Date**: 2024-05-24
**Threat Level**: HIGH

### Company Overview
Founded: 1999 | Funding: Profitable/Bootstrapped | Employees: 100+
HQ: Bangkok, Thailand | Key markets: Thai high school students (ม.ปลาย), TCAS preparation

### Product Analysis
Core value proposition: The ultimate community and information hub for Thai teenagers and university admission.
Target ICP: ม.4 - ม.6 students preparing for university entrance.
Key features: TCAS news, active forums, educational articles, Pre-TCAS mock exams.
Unique capabilities: Massive existing community trust; "Pre-TCAS" is seen as a rite of passage.
Notable gaps: The free exam practice experience is often buried within articles or forum threads. Lack of a lightweight, daily-habit interactive practice tool.

### Pricing
Model: Freemium / Pay-per-exam
Entry: Free (articles, forums) | Growth: Pre-TCAS mock exams (~200-500 THB) | Enterprise: N/A
Free tier: Yes — basic quizzes but mixed with heavy community content.

### Positioning
Category: Teen Portal & EdTech Community
Primary message: Everything you need to get into your dream university.
Differentiation claims: Largest student community, fastest TCAS updates.

### Competitive Assessment
Where we win: Clean, distraction-free UI; instant access with zero login; fast mobile experience.
Where they win: Brand recognition, comprehensive TCAS news, massive user volume.
Contested ground: Free online exam practice.

### Battlecard
Our strongest differentiators vs this competitor:
1. **Zero friction**: No login required, no pop-up ads blocking the main content, instant start.
2. **Focused UX**: Dek-D is cluttered with novels, lifestyle news, and forums; we are 100% focused on exam practice.

Common objections when we lose to them:
1. "Dek-D has more users and official news." → We are a focused practice tool, not a news site. Use both for maximum advantage.

### Strategic Recommendation
**UI/UX Weakness to Exploit**: Dek-D's interface is cluttered and ad-heavy. We must build a hyper-minimal, distraction-free interface that loads instantly on mobile devices. 
**SEO Content Gap**: Dek-D ranks for broad terms. We should target hyper-specific, action-oriented long-tail keywords like `ฝึกทำข้อสอบ TGAT ออนไลน์ ฟรี ทันที` (Practice TGAT exam online free instantly) and `เฉลยข้อสอบคณิตศาสตร์ ONET ปีล่าสุด ไม่ต้องสมัคร` (Latest ONET math exam answers without registration).

## COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE REPORT
**Competitor**: Trueplookpanya | **Date**: 2024-05-24
**Threat Level**: HIGH

### Company Overview
Founded: 2007 | Funding: Backed by True Corporation | Employees: 200+
HQ: Bangkok, Thailand | Key markets: K-12 Education in Thailand

### Product Analysis
Core value proposition: Massive repository of free educational content, videos, and exams.
Target ICP: K-12 students and teachers across Thailand.
Key features: VOD lessons, extensive question banks, career guides.
Unique capabilities: Unmatched volume of content and corporate telecom backing.
Notable gaps: Navigation is incredibly complex. Exams feel like corporate assessments rather than engaging practice.

### Pricing
Model: Free (Ad-supported / Corporate CSR)
Entry: Free | Growth: N/A | Enterprise: N/A
Free tier: Yes, but heavily gated behind TrueID login.

### Positioning
Category: Corporate Educational CSR / Content Portal
Primary message: Cultivating wisdom for Thai youth.
Differentiation claims: Backed by the largest telecom, most comprehensive content bank.

### Competitive Assessment
Where we win: Speed, agility, modern UI, zero login barrier.
Where they win: Sheer volume of questions, video explanations.
Contested ground: Search engine rankings for subject-specific questions.

### Battlecard
Our strongest differentiators vs this competitor:
1. **No Login Wall**: Trueplookpanya aggressively pushes TrueID login. We let students practice immediately.
2. **Modern Mobile UX**: Their mobile experience feels like a desktop site squeezed into a phone. Ours is mobile-first.

Common objections when we lose to them:
1. "True has video explanations." → We provide concise, text-based instant feedback which is faster for quick review sessions.

### Strategic Recommendation
**UI/UX Weakness to Exploit**: Corporate bloat, slow load times, and mandatory login. We will win by offering a "click-and-play" experience.
**SEO Content Gap**: They dominate broad educational terms. We can exploit gaps in newly updated, year-specific micro-exams using structured data (Schema.org/Quiz) so our questions appear directly in Google search snippets. Target: `ข้อสอบ PAT1 ปี 66 พร้อมเฉลยละเอียด โหลดเร็ว` (PAT1 2023 exam with detailed answers, fast loading).

## COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE REPORT
**Competitor**: Kru.co.th (and similar Tutor/Teacher portals) | **Date**: 2024-05-24
**Threat Level**: MEDIUM

### Company Overview
Founded: Various | Funding: Small business/Tutor-driven | Employees: 1-10
HQ: Thailand | Key markets: Specific subject tutoring

### Product Analysis
Core value proposition: Expert-led tutoring and downloadable exam resources.
Target ICP: Students looking for specific subject mastery or paid courses.
Key features: Course selling, PDF exam downloads, tutor profiles.
Unique capabilities: High-quality, curated questions by subject matter experts.
Notable gaps: Very poor interactive web experience. Mostly reliant on static PDFs.

### Pricing
Model: E-commerce (selling courses/books)
Entry: Free PDF teasers | Growth: Paid online courses | Enterprise: N/A
Free tier: Free PDF downloads.

### Positioning
Category: Tutoring Marketplace & Resources
Primary message: Learn from the best teachers.
Differentiation claims: Quality of instruction and expert reputation.

### Competitive Assessment
Where we win: Interactive digital experience, automated grading, progress tracking.
Where they win: Depth of expert explanation.
Contested ground: Students searching for past papers.

### Battlecard
Our strongest differentiators vs this competitor:
1. **Interactive vs Static**: They offer PDFs that students have to print. We offer an interactive app with instant grading.

Common objections when we lose to them:
1. "I want to print it out." → Our app tracks your weak points automatically; paper can't do that.

### Strategic Recommendation
**UI/UX Weakness to Exploit**: Lack of interactivity. The entire "download PDF, print, take exam, manually check answers" loop is broken. We automate this loop.
**SEO Content Gap**: Exploit the pain point of downloading files. Target keywords like `ทำข้อสอบ TGAT ออนไลน์ ไม่ต้องโหลด PDF` (Take TGAT exam online, no PDF download required).

## COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE REPORT
**Competitor**: Quizlet TH | **Date**: 2024-05-24
**Threat Level**: LOW / MONITOR

### Company Overview
Founded: 2005 | Funding: $60M+ | Employees: 200+
HQ: San Francisco, USA | Key markets: Global students, language learners

### Product Analysis
Core value proposition: The easiest way to practice and master what you're learning.
Target ICP: Students memorizing vocabulary or concepts.
Key features: Flashcards, spaced repetition, matching games.
Unique capabilities: World-class UI/UX, massive user-generated content library.
Notable gaps: Not optimized for Thai standardized test formats (long reading passages, complex math equations).

### Pricing
Model: Freemium
Entry: Free | Growth: Quizlet Plus (~$35/year) | Enterprise: N/A
Free tier: Yes, but core features like offline mode and detailed explanations are paywalled.

### Positioning
Category: Global EdTech / Study Tools
Primary message: Master any subject with flashcards.
Differentiation claims: AI-powered study modes, global community.

### Competitive Assessment
Where we win: Localized content format (ONET/TCAS style), entirely free.
Where they win: Polished app experience, gamification, cross-platform sync.
Contested ground: Students looking for quick study tools.

### Battlecard
Our strongest differentiators vs this competitor:
1. **Exam Simulation**: Quizlet is for memorization. Thai Exam Hub is for realistic exam simulation.

Common objections when we lose to them:
1. "Quizlet has a better mobile app." → We don't require an app install (saving phone storage), and we match the actual format of the Thai exams.

### Strategic Recommendation
**UI/UX Weakness to Exploit**: Quizlet's UI is optimized for short flashcards. Thai exams have long reading comprehension passages and complex math. We will design our UI specifically to handle Thai exam typography and long-form questions comfortably on mobile.
**SEO Content Gap**: Target simulation keywords over memorization keywords. Target `จำลองสอบ TCAS เสมือนจริง จับเวลา` (Realistic TCAS mock exam with timer) to capture students looking for exam practice rather than flashcards.
